# T21: Short-Shift Bilinear Incidence Gate

**Status:** exact Diophantine expansion of the T20 short-shift task; no incidence or cancellation estimate is claimed.  
**Purpose:** expose the finite arithmetic objects that must be bounded to obtain the weighted fourth-moment saving.

---

## 1. One pair of Type-II pieces

Let two sector-restricted bilinear pieces have the form
\[
\mathcal B_1(\alpha)
=
\sum_{\substack{m\sim M_1\\n\sim L_1}}
a_{m,n}e(\alpha mn),
\qquad
\mathcal B_2(\alpha)
=
\sum_{\substack{u\sim M_2\\v\sim L_2}}
b_{u,v}e(\alpha uv),
\]
with
\[
M_1L_1\asymp X,
\qquad
M_2L_2\asymp X.
\]

The coefficients may include divisor weights, logarithms, Möbius factors, and congruence restrictions inherited from a valid Vaughan/Heath–Brown decomposition. They are not assumed nonnegative.

Their product is
\[
\mathcal B_1(\alpha)\mathcal B_2(\alpha)
=
\sum_t C(t)e(\alpha t),
\]
where
\[
C(t)
=
\sum_{\substack{m,n,u,v\\mn+uv=t}}
a_{m,n}b_{u,v}.
\]

This is the additive coefficient sequence entering T19.

---

## 2. Exact shifted-correlation expansion

For a shift \(h\),
\[
\mathcal C_C(h)
:=
\sum_tC(t)\overline{C(t-h)}.
\]

Substitution of the coefficient definition gives the exact eight-variable identity
\[
\boxed{
\mathcal C_C(h)
=
\sum_{\substack{m,n,u,v\\m',n',u',v'\\
mn+uv-m'n'-u'v'=h}}
 a_{m,n}b_{u,v}
 \overline{a_{m',n'}b_{u',v'}}.
}
\]

Thus the T20 short-shift problem is a weighted count/cancellation problem for the near-collision equation
\[
\boxed{
mn+uv-m'n'-u'v'=h.
}
\]

There is no hidden product weight. The shift is additive in the final output coordinate.

## 3. Sector conservation kills non-sector shifts

For an exact sector pair \((\sigma_j,\tau_j)\), every additive coefficient satisfies
\[
C(t)=0
\qquad\text{unless}\qquad
 t\equiv j\pmod6.
\]

Therefore
\[
\boxed{
\mathcal C_C(h)=0
\quad\text{unless}\quad h\equiv0\pmod6.
}
\]

This matches the sector-preserving Fejér kernel from T11–T12. The T20 finite short-shift sum may therefore be rewritten as
\[
\sum_{|h|\le H}
\widehat W^{\rm sm}_{\mathfrak F,K}(h)\mathcal C_C(h)
=
\sum_{|r|\le H/6}
\widehat W^{\rm sm}_{\mathfrak F,K}(6r)\mathcal C_C(6r).
\]

The mod-6 structure reduces the shift family but does not itself create cancellation in the surviving correlations.

## 4. Diagonal families

At \(h=0\), the literal tuple diagonal
\[
(m,n,u,v)=(m',n',u',v')
\]
contributes
\[
\mathcal D_{\rm tuple}
=
\sum_{m,n,u,v}|a_{m,n}|^2|b_{u,v}|^2.
\]

There are also product-collision families in which
\[
mn=m'n',
\qquad
uv=u'v',
\]
without equality of the individual factor pairs. Their total contribution depends on the divisor structure and coefficient supports.

Accordingly, even the diagonal coefficient energy
\[
\mathcal C_C(0)=\sum_t|C(t)|^2
\]
is an additive energy of two bilinear images. It cannot be replaced by the literal tuple diagonal without a separately proved collision bound.

For \(h\ne0\), the literal tuple diagonal disappears, but near-collision families remain.

## 5. A finite weighted incidence functional

Let the smooth far-minor weights from T20 be
\[
\lambda_r
:=
\widehat W^{\rm sm}_{\mathfrak F,K}(6r),
\qquad |r|\le R_H:=\lfloor H/6\rfloor.
\]

Define
\[
\mathfrak I_H(a,b;\lambda)
:=
\sum_{|r|\le R_H}
\lambda_r
\sum_{\substack{m,n,u,v\\m',n',u',v'\\
mn+uv-m'n'-u'v'=6r}}
 a_{m,n}b_{u,v}
 \overline{a_{m',n'}b_{u',v'}}.
\]

Then the short-shift piece of the T19 functional is exactly
\[
\boxed{
\sum_{|h|\le H}
\widehat W^{\rm sm}_{\mathfrak F,K}(h)\mathcal C_C(h)
=
\mathfrak I_H(a,b;\lambda).
}
\]

This is the finite theorem target generated by the entire Fejér/OFI setup.

## 6. Sufficient incidence/cancellation targets

A strong absolute-value target is
\[
\boxed{
\sum_{|r|\le R_H}
|\lambda_r|
\left|
\mathcal C_C(6r)
\right|
\ll X^{3-\eta}.
}
\]

A more structured target can separate the central correlation:
\[
|\lambda_0|\,\mathcal C_C(0)
+
\sum_{0<|r|\le R_H}
|\lambda_r|\,|\mathcal C_C(6r)|
\ll X^{3-\eta}.
\]

Either bound, together with the T20 collar and Fourier-tail ledger, supplies the T18 fourth-moment estimate for this pair of bilinear pieces.

No statement here claims such a bound. In particular, generic divisor bounds alone do not visibly force a power saving at the required scale.

## 7. Where a valid analytic tool could enter

The near-collision equation may be attacked through a properly stated method such as:

\[
\text{Cauchy--Schwarz plus differencing},
\qquad
\text{large-sieve spacing},
\qquad
\text{dispersion/bilinear forms},
\qquad
\text{or a spectral/transfer estimate tied to this exact sum}.
\]

Any OFI, Riesz, semigroup, or transfer-operator input must output a quantitative bound for \(\mathfrak I_H\), its off-diagonal component, or a stronger precursor that demonstrably implies one. A window identity alone only specifies the coefficients \(\lambda_r\).

## 8. Immediate next subproblem

The next narrow target is to choose one genuine Type-II range and prove a nontrivial bound for
\[
mn+uv-m'n'-u'v'=6r,
qquad |r|\le R_H,
\]
with the actual decomposition coefficients and sector restrictions retained.

This is the first place where the framework has reduced the far-minor density-one branch to a finite family of explicit arithmetic incidence sums.
