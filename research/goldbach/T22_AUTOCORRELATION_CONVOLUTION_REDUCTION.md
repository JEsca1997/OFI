# T22: Autocorrelation-Convolution Reduction

**Status:** exact algebraic factorization of the T21 eight-variable incidence functional; no correlation estimate is claimed.  
**Purpose:** reduce the short-shift correlation of a sum of two bilinear images to a convolution of two four-variable shifted-product correlations.

---

## 1. Separate the two bilinear image sequences

For the Type-II pieces in T21, define
\[
A(x):=\sum_{\substack{m\sim M_1,\ n\sim L_1\\mn=x}}a_{m,n},
\qquad
B(y):=\sum_{\substack{u\sim M_2,\ v\sim L_2\\uv=y}}b_{u,v}.
\]

Then
\[
\mathcal B_1(\alpha)=\sum_xA(x)e(\alpha x),
\qquad
\mathcal B_2(\alpha)=\sum_yB(y)e(\alpha y).
\]

The additive coefficient sequence from T21 is exactly
\[
C=A*B,
\qquad
C(t)=\sum_xA(x)B(t-x).
\]

---

## 2. Reflection and autocorrelation notation

For a finitely supported complex sequence \(F\), write
\[
F^\ast(n):=\overline{F(-n)}.
\]

Its additive autocorrelation is
\[
\mathcal R_F(h)
:=(F*F^\ast)(h)
=
\sum_xF(x)\overline{F(x-h)}.
\]

In particular,
\[
\mathcal C_C(h)=\mathcal R_C(h).
\]

---

## 3. Exact autocorrelation-convolution identity

Since \(C=A*B\), associativity and commutativity of convolution imply
\[
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal R_C
&=(A*B)*(A*B)^\ast\\
&=(A*B)*(A^\ast*B^\ast)\\
&=(A*A^\ast)*(B*B^\ast).
\end{aligned}
\]

Thus
\[
\boxed{
\mathcal C_C(h)
=
(\mathcal R_A*\mathcal R_B)(h)
=
\sum_{r\in\mathbb Z}\mathcal R_A(r)\mathcal R_B(h-r).
}
\]

This is an exact reduction; no inequality has been used.

---

## 4. Four-variable shifted-product correlations

The two factors are
\[
\boxed{
\mathcal R_A(r)
=
\sum_{\substack{m,n,m',n'\\mn-m'n'=r}}
a_{m,n}\overline{a_{m',n'}},
}
\]
and
\[
\boxed{
\mathcal R_B(s)
=
\sum_{\substack{u,v,u',v'\\uv-u'v'=s}}
b_{u,v}\overline{b_{u',v'}}.
}
\]

Consequently, T21's equation
\[
mn+uv-m'n'-u'v'=h
\]
is reorganized as
\[
(mn-m'n')+(uv-u'v')=h.
\]

The eight-variable near-collision is therefore a convolution of two shifted-product correlation families.

---

## 5. Exact insertion into the T20 short-shift functional

Let
\[
\lambda_r=\widehat W^{\rm sm}_{\mathfrak F,K}(6r),
\qquad |r|\le R_H.
\]

Then T21's finite functional is
\[
\begin{aligned}
\mathfrak I_H(a,b;\lambda)
&=
\sum_{|r|\le R_H}\lambda_r\mathcal C_C(6r)\\
&=
\boxed{
\sum_{|r|\le R_H}\lambda_r
\sum_{s\in\mathbb Z}
\mathcal R_A(s)\mathcal R_B(6r-s).
}
\end{aligned}
\]

Equivalently, with the finite kernel
\[
\Lambda_H(t):=
\begin{cases}
\lambda_{t/6},&t\in6\mathbb Z,\ |t|\le6R_H,\\
0,&\text{otherwise},
\end{cases}
\]
we have
\[
\mathfrak I_H(a,b;\lambda)
=
\sum_{s,t}\Lambda_H(s+t)\mathcal R_A(s)\mathcal R_B(t).
\]

This is a bilinear pairing of two shifted-product correlation sequences.

---

## 6. Sector implications

Whenever the individual bilinear image sequences have fixed mod-\(6\) support,
\[
A(x)=0\ \text{unless}\ x\equiv a_0\pmod6,
\qquad
B(y)=0\ \text{unless}\ y\equiv b_0\pmod6,
\]
then
\[
\mathcal R_A(r)=0,
\quad
\mathcal R_B(r)=0
\qquad(r\not\equiv0\pmod6).
\]

In that case the entire sum in Section 5 runs only over
\[
s,t\in6\mathbb Z.
\]

For a decomposition whose individual pieces are not separately sector-pure, this simplification may only be imposed after the full sector projection is restored. The sector condition must not be silently moved through a combinatorial decomposition.

---

## 7. What must now be proved

The full-circle identity does not give a saving by itself. A sufficient short-shift estimate is
\[
\boxed{
\sum_{|r|\le R_H}|\lambda_r|
\sum_s
|\mathcal R_A(s)|\,|\mathcal R_B(6r-s)|
\ll X^{3-\eta}.
}
\]

A sharper route is allowed to exploit cancellation in the signed correlation sequences before absolute values are taken:
\[
\boxed{
\left|
\sum_{|r|\le R_H}\lambda_r
\sum_s\mathcal R_A(s)\mathcal R_B(6r-s)
\right|
\ll X^{3-\eta}.
}
\]

The latter is the exact quantity generated by the Fourier calculation and is potentially much smaller than the absolute-value majorant. It requires a real cancellation mechanism.

---

## 8. Natural next arithmetic gate

The next theorem is no longer eight-variable by default. It is a weighted convolution estimate for the two four-variable product-difference correlations:
\[
mn-m'n'=s,
\qquad
uv-u'v'=t,
qquad s+t=6r.
\]

Any dispersion, large-sieve, Kloosterman/spectral, or OFI-derived mechanism must be stated as a bound for \(\mathcal R_A\), \(\mathcal R_B\), or their weighted convolution above. A spline identity only selects the kernel \(\lambda_r\); it does not bound these correlations automatically.

---

## 9. Outcome

T22 replaces the raw eight-variable incidence condition with the exact hierarchy
\[
\text{bilinear product-difference correlations}
\to
\text{their weighted convolution}
\to
\text{T20 short-shift functional}
\to
\text{T18 density-one bound}.
\]

This is the tightest finite arithmetic reduction currently justified by the Fejér/OFI framework.
